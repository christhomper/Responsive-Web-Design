Peppermint Chip Ice Cream

Treat yourself to the refreshing flavor of Peppermint Chip Ice Cream.

This recipe promises a cooling blend of creamy texture and invigorating mint enhanced by dark chocolate chips scattered throughout. Whether you're a seasoned ice cream maker or just starting out, this recipe's straightforward instructions and common ingredients make it a breeze to create your own frozen delight.

Ingredients

  • Cream Mixture:
    • 1 ½ cups whole milk
    • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
    • ½ cup sugar
    • pinch of salt

  • Egg Yolk Mixture:
    • 6 egg yolks
    • ¼ cup sugar
    • 1 tbsp peppermint extract

  • 10 peppermints crushed

  • ¾ cup dark chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. In a medium sized saucepan add all cream mixture ingredients and turn heat to low to begin heating.

  2. While cream is beginning to heat, add all ingredients for the egg yolk mixture to a small mixing bowl and whisk vigorously for about 1 minute. Set aside.

  3. Turn up heat for cream mixture to medium high and stir constantly to ensure cream does not burn. You want to bring this mixture almost to a boil, where steam is beginning to roll off the top. Once you have reached this point, remove from heat.

  4. While whisking the egg yolk mixture, take ¼ cup of the hot cream, and slowly add it to the yolks. Repeat this 3 more times. This tempers the egg yolks and keeps them from cooking when added to the saucepan.

  5. Add egg yolk/cream mixture back to the saucepan with the rest of the cream. Turning to medium heat, stir until the ice cream base begins to thicken, again bringing it almost to a boil. Remove from heat and allow to cool completely. I will often make the base the night before I want to churn the ice cream.

  6. The next day, churn ice cream in ice cream maker according to manufacturers instructions. Once the ice cream has finished churning fold in the chocolate chips and crushed peppermint candies.

  7. If you prefer soft serve, enjoy immediately, or place ice cream in freezer for a few hours for a more solid treat.

Nutritional Information

Yields 8 servings
Serving size ½ cup

  • 26g fat

  • 42g carbohydrate

  • 4g protein
