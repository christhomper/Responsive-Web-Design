Broccoli Cheddar Soup

Experience the flavors of Panera Bread's famous broccoli cheddar soup with this Instant Pot rendition.

In this pressure cooker adaptation, chopped broccoli, shredded carrots, and celery are delicately simmered in a luxuriously smooth cheese sauce. I am confident that this homemade version rivals or even surpasses what you could enjoy at any restaurant. Consider giving it a try for dinner this week!

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp butter

  • 1 white onion

  • 3-4 cloves minced garlic

  • 1 cup celery chopped

  • 1 cup shredded carrots

  • 1 pound bag frozen broccoli florets

  • 4 cups chicken broth

  • 1/4 cup butter

  • 1/4 cup corn starch

  • 2 cups milk

  • 1 cup light cream

  • 8 oz grated extra sharp cheddar

  • 1 block cream cheese

  • 1/2 tsp salt

  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

  • 1/2 tsp paprika

  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper

Instructions

  1. Begin by setting your Instant Pot to the saute function and melting 1 tablespoon of butter. Once melted, add the diced onion and minced garlic and saute for 3 minutes.

  2. Introduce celery, carrots, broccoli, and chicken broth to the Instant Pot. Cover the pot, lock the lid, and ensure the valve is set to "sealing." Adjust the manual (or pressure cook) button to 1 minute.

  3. As the pot heats up and reaches pressure, melt 1/4 cup of butter in a medium sized skillet on the stove over medium heat. Add the corn starch, milk, cream cheese(cubed) and light cream until a creamy mixture forms and turn heat to low. Continue stirring the cream cheese sauce until a smooth consistency is reached. Also, grate the cheddar cheese and set aside.

  4. When the Instant Pot timer beeps, indicating the completion of the cooking time, let the pot naturally release for 5 minutes. Then, move the valve to "venting" and remove the lid once the pressure has fully released.

  5. Switch the Instant Pot back to the saute function. Whisk one cup of broth from the soup into the cream cheese sauce and stir until combined. Incorporate the entire cream cheese sauce mixture into the pot and stir until the soup thickens. Follow by stirring in the cheddar cheese until it melts. Season with salt, black pepper, paprika, and cayenne pepper.

  6. Ladle the soup into bowls and serve. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. Enjoy!

Nutritional Information

Yields 6 servings
Serving size 2 cups
  • 555 calories

  • 43g fat

  • 22g carbohydrate

  • 19g protein
