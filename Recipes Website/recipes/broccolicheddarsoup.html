Broccoli Cheddar Soup

Experience the flavors of Panera Bread's famous broccoli cheddar soup with this Instant Pot rendition.

In this pressure cooker adaptation, chopped broccoli, shredded carrots, and celery are delicately simmered in a luxuriously smooth cheese sauce. I am confident that this homemade version rivals or even surpasses what you could enjoy at any restaurant. Consider giving it a try for dinner this week!

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp butter

  • 1 white onion

  • 1 cup celery chopped

  • 1 cup shredded carrots

  • 1 pound bag frozen broccoli florets

  • 4 cups chicken broth

  • 1/4 cup butter

  • 1/4 cup corn starch

  • 2 cups milk

  • 1 cup light cream

  • 8 oz grated extra sharp cheddar

  • 1 block cream cheese

  • 1/2 tsp salt

  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

  • 1/2 tsp paprika

  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper

Instructions

  1. Begin by setting your Instant Pot to the saute function ("more") and melting 1 tablespoon of butter. Once melted, add the diced onion and saute for 3 minutes.

  2. Introduce celery, carrots, broccoli, and chicken broth to the Instant Pot. Cover the pot, lock the lid, and ensure the valve is set to "sealing." Adjust the manual (or pressure cook) button to 1 minute.

  3. As the pot heats up and reaches pressure, melt 1/4 cup of butter in a microwave-safe dish (or on the stove). Gradually whisk in flour until a creamy mixture forms. Set aside. Optionally, heat the milk to expedite the process. Also, grate the cheddar and set it aside.

  4. When the Instant Pot timer beeps, indicating the completion of the cooking time, let the pot sit for 5 minutes. Then, move the valve to "venting" and remove the lid.

  5. Switch the Instant Pot back to the saute function. Whisk one cup of broth from the soup into the butter and flour mixture. Incorporate the entire mixture into the pot and stir until the soup thickens. Add the pre-heated milk and stir until heated through. Follow by stirring in the cheese until it melts. Season with salt and pepper. For a thicker consistency, mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of cold water in a small bowl and stir the cornstarch slurry into the pot.

  6. Ladle the soup into bowls and serve. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week.
