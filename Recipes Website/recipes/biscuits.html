Buttermilk Biscuits

Indulge in the simple joy of homemade Buttermilk Biscuits.

With a perfect balance of fluffiness and buttery flavor, these biscuits are a treat for your taste buds. Whether enjoyed on their own or paired with your favorite dishes, they are a versatile and comforting addition to any meal.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups flour(I use unbleached all purpose)

  • 5 tsp baking powder

  • 1 ½ tsp salt

  • ½ tsp baking soda

  • 1 stick (½ cup) chilled butter

  • 1 ¼ C cold buttermilk (I use low-fat)

Instructions

  1. Cut the stick of butter into small cubes and place it back in the fridge to keep it chilled until needed.

  2. Sift flour, baking powder, salt, and baking soda into a large bowl.

  3. Cut chilled butter into the flour mixture until the butter is the size of a pea, using a pastry cutter or dough blender.

  4. Pour in buttermilk and mix together with a spoon. If the dough is not coming together, use your hands to finish forming the dough.

  5. Pour the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Roll the dough to a thickness of ½ inch using a rolling pin.

  6. Using a round biscuit cutter, cut out as many biscuits as you can. Form a new ball with the leftover dough, roll it out to ½ inch thickness, and cut out more biscuits. Repeat until all the dough is used up.

  7. Bake the biscuits at 425°F for about 20 minutes.

    8. Pro Tip: These biscuits freeze well. When craving a homemade biscuit, pull them out of the freezer and bake in a preheated oven for 20 minutes.

    One of my favorite ways to enjoy these buttery, fluffy biscuits is with homemade sausage gravy.
