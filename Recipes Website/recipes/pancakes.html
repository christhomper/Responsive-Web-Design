Buttermilk Pancakes

Start your day on a delicious note with Buttermilk Pancakes.

Light, fluffy, and with a hint of tanginess from the buttermilk, these pancakes are a breakfast classic that never disappoint. Made with simple ingredients, this recipe ensures a perfect balance of sweetness and a golden, slightly crisp exterior. Whether you top them with maple syrup, fresh berries, or a dollop of whipped cream, these pancakes are a delightful way to kick off your morning.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour (I use unbleached all purpose)

  • 2 tsp baking powder

  • ½ tsp salt

  • ½ tsp baking soda

  • 3 Tbsp sugar

  • 2 ¼ cups buttermilk

  • 2 eggs

  • 4 Tbsp butter melted

Instructions

  1. Preheat griddle. Whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

  2. Add eggs, buttermilk, and melted butter to the flour mixture and whisk until there are no lumps.

  3. Pour ¼ cup batter onto the griddle, repeating until griddle is full. Wait to flip pancakes until the tops are nice and bubbly (about 2-3 minutes). Flip and cook for additional 30 seconds to 1 minute.

  4. Serve with butter, maple syrup, fresh fruit, and/or whipped topping.
