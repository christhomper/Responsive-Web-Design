Creamy Tuscan Chicken Pasta

Capture the essence of the Tuscan countryside right in your home with a serving of velvety, al dente penne pasta.

In this recipe, the focal points are the succulent chicken thighs, garlic, and Asiago cheese. Although cream cheese adds a non-traditional but rich dimension to the sauce, the inclusion of baby spinach and sun-dried tomatoes brings a harmonious balance to the dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs

  • 2 tbsp olive oil

  • 2 tbsp butter

  • 1/2 white onion diced

  • 2 garlic cloves minced

  • 2 cups chicken broth

  • 2 cups light cream

  • 8 oz sun-dried tomatoes in oil, drained and chopped

  • 2 tsp Italian seasoning

  • 1 box penne pasta, 14.5 oz

  • 1 cup grated Asiago cheese

  • 1 block cream cheese

  • 1 small box Baby Spinach 5 oz

  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

  • 1/2 tsp salt

  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes

Instructions

  1. Place your chicken thighs on a cutting board and generously season one side with salt and black pepper.

  2. Choose the Saute function and set it to High. Press Start and wait until the display indicates Hot. Add olive oil to the inner pot.

  3. Add the chicken to the pot and cook for 3-4 minutes or until lightly browned. Set aside the cooked chicken and, once cooled, cut it into small pieces.

  4. In the pot, add the butter and onion, cooking for 2 minutes. Then, add garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute until fragrant. Press Cancel.

  5. Incorporate light cream, chicken stock, sun-dried tomatoes, Italian seasoning, salt, pepper, red pepper flakes and penne pasta. Stir until the pasta is fully immersed in the liquid.

  6. Add the chopped chicken thighs. Seal the lid for pressure cooking.

  7. Opt for Pressure Cook, High, and set the time for 5 minutes (00:05). Press Start.

  8. Once the program is complete, perform a Quick Release of pressure. Press Cancel and remove the lid after the pressure valve drops.

  9. Add freshly grated Asiago cheese, cream cheese, and baby spinach.

  10. Stir the pasta until the cheese is melted, and the spinach has wilted (about 1 minute).

  11. Serve, and garnish as desired.
