Benihana Style Fried Rice

Indulge in the irresistible flavors of Benihana with this Fried Rice copycat recipe.

Succulent shrimp, perfectly cooked rice, and a medley of fresh vegetables come together in a symphony of savory perfection. Recreate the magic of Benihana right at home with this delicious and satisfying dish that captures the essence of their iconic shrimp fried rice. Elevate your culinary skills and treat your taste buds to a taste of restaurant-quality goodness!

Ingredients

  • Shrimp:
    • 1/2 pound of shrimp (or any preferred protein substitute)
    • 1/2 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
    • 1 teaspon sesame oil

  • Rice:
    • Avoid using freshly steamed rice! Opt for rice that's at least a day old to prevent excess moisture. Too much moisture can result in soggy and mushy fried rice.
    • 6 cups cooked

  • Garlic Butter:
    • 1/2 cup softened butter
    • 3 teaspoons soy sauce
    • 3 garlic cloves grated

  • Sesame Oil:
    • While my preferred oil is sesame oil, you can also use canola or vegetable oil for a more neutral flavor profile.

  • 4 medium carrots diced

  • 1/2 white onion diced

  • 3 scallions chopped

  • 2-3 eggs

  • 3 garlic cloves minced

  • Garlic Butter (see above)

  • 1/2 tablespoon soy sauce

  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar

Instructions

  1. Prepare your rice the night before and leave it covered in the fridge to dry overnight or for up to a day for optimal results.

  2. Soften 1/2 cup of butter and grate 3 cloves of garlic. Add light soy sauce, mix well, and refrigerate until firm and ready to use.

  3. Marinate your chosen protein with soy sauce, salt, black pepper, and 1/2 tsp of neutral oil. Chop carrots, onions, and scallions, and mince garlic. Set aside.

  4. Heat 1 tbsp of oil in a pan over high heat. Cook your protein, incorporating 1 tbsp of garlic butter for an extra burst of flavor. Remove and set aside.

  5. In the same pan, heat 3 tbsp of oil. Briefly fry garlic for 15 seconds, followed by carrots, onions, and scallions for 1-2 minutes. Add the rice and continue frying, separating and combining until relatively dry (2-3 minutes).

  6. Push the rice to one side and add 1 tbsp of oil. Fry 2 eggs until they are mostly done, then mix them into the rice. Season with light soy sauce, black pepper, sugar, and 2 tbsp of garlic butter. Mix and fry for an additional 1-2 minutes. Enjoy!
