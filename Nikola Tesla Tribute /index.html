Nikola Tesla

The Life and Achievements of Nikola Tesla

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia)
  • 1884 - Moves to New York City to work with Thomas Edison
  • 1891 - Patents the Tesla coil and becomes a naturalized American citizen
  • 1893 - Demonstrates wireless power transmission at the Chicago World's Fair
  • 1896 - Begins work on wireless communication and radio waves
  • 1901 - Patents a method for transmitting electrical energy without wires
  • 1943 - Passes away in New York City, leaving behind a legacy of innovation

Legacy and Impact

Nikola Tesla's work laid the foundation for many modern technologies, including alternating current (AC) electrical systems and wireless communication. His inventions and ideas continue to shape the world of science and engineering.
