Vitamin A

Vitamin A is essential for vision, immune function, and skin health.

Deficiency can lead to vision problems and reduced immunity.

Recommended sources: carrots, sweet potatoes, and kale.
  • Helps form and maintain healthy teeth, skeletal and soft tissue
  • Helps produce the pigments in the retina of the eye
Vitamin B

Vitamin B refers to a complex of eight vitamins crucial for cellular metabolism.

Each vitamin in this group has unique functions.

Recommended sources: whole grains, meat, poultry, fish, beans, peas, and nuts.
  • Helps form red blood cells
  • Aids in energy release from proteins
Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)

Thiamine is essential for glucose metabolism and plays a key role in nerve, muscle, and heart function.

Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin)

Riboflavin helps break down proteins, fats, and carbohydrates. It plays a vital role in maintaining the body's energy supply.

Vitamin B3 (Niacin)

Niacin aids in the metabolism of food and is essential for healthy skin, nerves, and digestion.

Vitamin B5 (Pantothenic Acid)

Pantothenic Acid is necessary for making blood cells, and it helps convert food into energy.

Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)

Pyridoxine is important for brain development and function. It helps the body produce the hormones serotonin and norepinephrine.

Vitamin B7 (Biotin)

Biotin is essential for the metabolism of carbohydrates and fats.

Vitamin B9 (Folic Acid)

Folic Acid plays a fundamental role in the formation of DNA and RNA. It's crucial during rapid growth phases, such as pregnancy.

Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)

Cobalamin is vital for the formation of red blood cells and helps in the proper functioning of the nervous system.

Vitamin C

Vitamin C, also known as ascorbic acid, is vital for collagen synthesis, wound healing, and antioxidant protection.

It also aids in the absorption of iron from plant-based foods.

Recommended sources: citrus fruits, strawberries, and bell peppers.
  • Boosts the immune system
  • Helps maintain skin, bones, and blood vessels
Vitamin D

Vitamin D is necessary for bone health and calcium absorption.

It also plays a role in immune system health.

Produced when skin is exposed to sunlight. Also found in fish and fortified dairy products.
  • Prevents rickets and osteomalacia
  • May play a role in mood regulation and immune function
Vitamin E

Vitamin E is a potent antioxidant that helps protect cells from oxidative stress.

It's also involved in immune function, skin health, and vision.

Recommended sources: almonds, spinach, and olive oil.
  • Supports immune function
  • Prevents inflammation
Vitamin K

Vitamin K is crucial for blood clotting and bone metabolism.

A deficiency can lead to excessive bleeding and osteoporosis.

Recommended sources: green leafy vegetables, broccoli, and brussels sprouts.
  • Supports bone health
  • Helps with blood clotting
